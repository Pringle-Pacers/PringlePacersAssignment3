IT Work

Interview Answers to Questions

Interviewee: Dat Tran

Occupation: ICT Operations manager

1. Please tell us about your IT work. What exactly do you do?

First I would like to give you a brief background about my profession. I have been with Ego Pharmaceutical for 14 years. I started as a senior system manager. Then I was promoted to the current position as the ICT operation Manager. My job is to ensure thatthe corporate IT infrastructure and network is running with minimal disruption to support the production and the business. To achieve this objective I would like to rely on my team members who are specialised in different IT areas to manage them. I receive their reports and identify areas that need attention and improvement. I provide guidance, support and courage to build teamwork. I research new technology suitable for the business and manufacture and plan for the implementation with minimal disruption.I also run my team meetings fortnightly and attend meetings as requested by other departments.

2. Please tell us about the industry you work in.

I work for a pharmaceutical company. The company manufactures dermatology products such as skin care products. Some of which are famous brands such as QV Face, illusion and derm-aid. This company is an Australian company that employs about 700 staff around the world. Each member of the company is proud of their work and proud to be an employee of the company. Likewise, this company looks after their staff well and contributes to their community with their ongoing ethics.

3. What other kinds of work do you have to do?

Apart from general activity that I have mentioned above, I also mentor my team members for self training as well as attending work related conferences. I do ICT budgeting, approve IT changes and requests, change implementation, appraising IT software and hardware, managing IT risk and IT securities, and sometimes coding for system integration.

4. Who are all the different people you interact with in your work? Please tell us about it.

I interact with different people from my team members to my supervisor, other managers in the same or different departments and user engineers, contractors, vendors, consultants and so on, depending on the work requirements from time to time.

5. Please tell us about your interactions with other IT professionals.

I also interact with IT professionals outside of the organisation. When I attend IT conferences to share knowledge or to explore new technology.

6. What about your interactions with clients or investors?

I do not interact with the investors directly. I sometimes interact with clients to cover the support desk team in case they are overloaded. In such situations, I need to minimise the use of highly technical jargon to avoid confusion or misunderstanding.

7. What aspects of your work do you spend most time on? Please tell us about these.

The aspect that I find where I spend most of my time, is actually managing the team to make sure they actually perform to their best to provide support to the business and the factory or manufacturer. To ensure that they might have adequate resources and support to perform and complete their task on the daily basis or on the set time for project targets.

8. Which aspects of your work do you find most challenging?

The aspects that I find most challenging is keeping your IT knowledge afresh,keeping IT documents updated and relevant and making IT changes, ensuring these changes are implemented smoothly.

9. Can you share an example of the work you do that best defines the IT industry?

The best experience I can give you which defines the IT industry actually is in automating the business model for self ordering taking towards production. It requires a lot of intrinsic knowledge between each component of the system. You need to know how the user or the cell representative actually submits the order by collecting the order from the pharmacy and submitting it to the cloud database. Then from there is actually translating the data into a format understandable by the other systems and then reinjecting it into an on-premises business system called SAP. And it's entirely automated and it actually helps save the amount of labour that individual people involved in the system have to spend on. And it's far more accurate and more timely than the manual recording the cell order. This will help with the knowledge of IT and the IT system infrastructure.

10. Finally, are there any thoughts you would like to give to university students graduating from IT courses?

To me, IT is the future. This discipline accelerates the growth of business, manufacturing, research, health, entertainment and in return releases humans from hard labour to have more time for doing interesting things such as more time with the family, which makes society better through living happier and more meaningful lives. So, do IT!
